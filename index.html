Life in Space

Have you ever wondered how people in space live when they are gone for collecting samples etc.

well if you didn't lets explore how!

Life in space is very different from life on Earth🌎.

Without strong gravity, astronauts float as they move, which makes everyday tasks like eating🍽️, drinking, and sleeping more complicated.

Space is quiet🔇 and is pitch black, which can feel peaceful but also lonely at times.

Astronauts may stay in space for weeks, months, or even up to a year, depending on their mission

Their muscles and bones🦴 can weaken over time, so regular exercise is must to stay healthy

So these are some things about how astronauts live in space👽

🌡️ Temperature in Space

Space is very different from Earth because it has no air.

This means heat🔥 cannot spread like it does on our planet.

Objects in direct sunlight can get extremely hot, sometimes hundreds of degrees Celsius.

In shadow or away from the Sun, temperatures can drop to hundreds of degrees below zero.

Astronauts and spacecraft must use special suits and insulation to protect against these extreme temperatures.

The Sun heats objects, but space itself is mostly cold and dark

The harsh temperatures of space remind us how special and fragile life on Earth is.

Space is full of extreme temperatures,

which makes life difficult there and shows how precious life on Earth is
